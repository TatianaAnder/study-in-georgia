Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about studying in Georgia

Why is Georgia considered a good destination?

Georgia is an increasingly popular destination for international students due to its high-quality education, affordable tuition, and low cost of living.

Are the degrees recognized internationally?

Yes, degrees from Georgian universities are internationally recognized. Medical degrees are recognized by WHO, WFME, WDOMS and more, allowing students to further their practice in a wide variety of countries.

⁠Are the programs in English?

While the official language is Georgian, many universities offer a wide variety of programs taught entirely in English, particularly for popular fields like medicine, business administration, and information technology. This makes it easier for international students to study without needing to learn the local language first.

How safe is the country?

Georgia is considered a very safe country with a low crime rate, consistently ranked among higher nations for global safety index.

When are admission deadlines?

There are no universal deadlines as they vary by university and program. Most Georgian universities have two admission intakes: the fall semester and the spring semester.

What documents are necessary?

Generally students will need Passport copy, Academic transcripts, Passport-sized photo.

What is the cost of living?

The cost of living in Georgia is very affordable compared to many other European countries. A student can comfortably live on about $400 to $800 per month(rent included).

⁠Do the universities offer scholarships?

Currently Georgian universities do not offer scholarships to international students, but some universities offer incentive based discounts that can be obtained depending on the GPA of the student.
